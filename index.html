Estimation comments (1)

$$\log E = \log C(S,W,P,N) - \log e(M,H,D)$$
  • $C$ is short-term (variable) costs
    (ignores capital and managerial inputs)
  • $E$ excludes transportation and food
  • $e$ is efficiency (managerial?)
  • $S$ is test scores, graduation rates, or college-bound

Political Economy

  • Cost function?
    • Costrell, Hanushek, and Loeb (2008)
    • Cost implies efficiency: $c(y,w)=\min_{x} \{w \cdot x | y = f(x) \}$
    • Estimates of average spending, not minimum costs

  • Competition?
    • Hernández-Murillo (JPubEcon, 2019)
    • Competition among public agents doesn't always promote efficiency
  • Hanushek
    • "Cost functions" do not tell us the cost of achieving any specified level of performace
    • They provide estimates of average spending for districts of given characteristics
    • They indicate how spending varies by these characteristics
    • Average spending is not the same as minimum efficient cost
  • Hernandez-Murillo
    • Competition among public agents is very different
    • Districts don't even control tax rates
    • What is their objective function and choice variables?
